# Industrial Enterprise Document Style

Use this guide when producing customer-facing technical reports, instrument selection reports, calculation books, compliance reviews, proposals, presentations, Word/PDF/Excel files, or Canva style directions.

## Style Target

The visual direction should feel close to established global industrial automation and electrical engineering enterprises such as ABB, Schneider Electric, Emerson, and Siemens: restrained, technical, international, grid-based, and evidence-led.

Do not copy their trademarks, logos, exact color systems, typefaces, proprietary templates, slogans, or page layouts. Do not imply official affiliation with any of these companies.

## Visual Principles

1. Use a white or very light gray page base with graphite or black body text.
2. Use one restrained accent color per document. Suitable accents include industrial blue, teal, green, red, or dark gray. Use accents only for section markers, thin rules, status tags, chart highlights, and callouts.
3. Avoid decorative gradients, bokeh, heavy shadows, rounded marketing cards, cartoon illustrations, and consumer-style templates.
4. Build pages on a strong grid with consistent margins, aligned headings, aligned tables, and clear revision metadata.
5. Prefer engineering content density: tables, specifications, calculation blocks, instrument tags, diagrams, error curves, compliance matrices, risk lists, and confirmation items.
6. Use thin separators, narrow accent bars, section numbers, and small status chips instead of large decorative blocks.
7. Keep cover pages formal: project name, document title, customer, document number, revision, date, prepared/reviewed/approved fields, and confidentiality level.
8. Use professional sans-serif typography. For Chinese, prefer Microsoft YaHei, Source Han Sans, DengXian, or similar. For English, prefer Arial, Aptos, Helvetica-like, or similar. Avoid ornate or casual fonts.
9. Charts must be clear and inspectable: include units, axis labels, legends, calculation conditions, and source notes. Avoid 3D charts.
10. Tables should be dense but readable: light gray header fill, thin borders, right-aligned numbers, units in column headers, and notes below the table.

## Status Colors

Use status colors sparingly and consistently:

- Green: `满足`, `推荐`, `合格`
- Amber: `需确认`, `边界风险`, `资料不足但可初步判断`
- Red: `不推荐`, `不适用`, `不合格`
- Gray: `资料未提供`, `未评价`, `不适用项`

Never use color alone as the only meaning carrier; pair it with text labels.

## Recommended Report Structure

For an instrument selection report:

1. Cover and revision record.
2. Executive conclusion with `推荐` / `可用但需确认` / `不推荐` / `不适用`.
3. Customer process conditions and missing parameters.
4. Source documents reviewed.
5. Instrument principle applicability.
6. Model and configuration review.
7. Range, pressure, temperature, material, output, power, Ex, and installation checks.
8. Calculations with formulas, substitutions, results, and engineering judgment.
9. Accuracy or uncertainty assessment, including curves or tables when required.
10. Risks, confirmation items, and final recommendation.

For a calculation book:

1. Scope and calculation basis.
2. Known conditions and assumptions.
3. Formula list.
4. Unit conversions.
5. Step-by-step calculations.
6. Result summary table.
7. Engineering judgment and boundary checks.

For a compliance review:

1. Applicable standard, version, and clauses.
2. Inspection or calibration data.
3. Allowable error and measured error comparison.
4. Pass/fail table.
5. Boundary risk and confirmation items.

For a customer technical reply:

1. Direct answer first.
2. Basis from customer data, datasheets, standards, or calculations.
3. Constraints and missing information.
4. Required customer/manufacturer confirmation.
5. Next action.

## Writing Tone

Use formal, engineering Chinese for Chinese deliverables. Prefer:

- `根据已提供资料...`
- `计算结果表明...`
- `该型号在当前工况下...`
- `资料中未找到明确依据，需厂家确认`
- `该结论仅基于当前资料，若工况参数变化需重新校核`

Avoid marketing phrases such as:

- `行业领先`
- `完美适配`
- `绝对可靠`
- `全面赋能`
- `革命性`
- `极致`
- `轻松解决`

## Canva Direction

If creating or guiding a Canva design without an approved company brand template, use this prompt direction:

`Create a clean industrial automation technical report style, inspired by global automation companies, with a white/light gray base, graphite typography, restrained engineering accent color, dense specification tables, process diagrams, calculation blocks, compliance matrices, and no logos or brand imitation.`

If Canva brand templates are available through a paid plan, search for internal templates related to technical reports, engineering proposals, industrial automation, commissioning reports, quality reports, or compliance documents, then adapt the selected template to the rules above.
